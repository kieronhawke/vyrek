<Callout tone="info" title="One reference">
This page collects every Hyrox station weight by category in one place. Bookmark it. Train against these numbers. The race-day weights are the only ones that matter; the gym version doesn't count.
</Callout>

The Hyrox standards are intentionally fixed. Same weights, same distance, same format, every event globally. That's the entire point of the brand: your time at one race is comparable to your time at every other. This is the current weight schedule by category.

## Open men

The default amateur men's category. The bulk of the recreational field.

| Station | Detail |
|---|---|
| 1. Ski erg | 1,000 m |
| 2. Sled push | 152 kg, 50 m total (25 + 25) |
| 3. Sled pull | 103 kg, 50 m total |
| 4. Burpee broad jump | 80 m, minimum 1 m per jump |
| 5. Rower | 1,000 m |
| 6. Farmer's carry | 24 kg per hand, 200 m |
| 7. Sandbag lunges | 20 kg, 100 m |
| 8. Wall ball | 100 reps, 6 kg to 10 ft / 3 m target |

Plus 8 × 1 km runs between stations.

## Doubles

Doubles uses open-category weights, split across two athletes. Each station has a split-and-tag structure:

- **Ski erg, rower:** One athlete works while the other rests in tag-style. 500 m each typical, but check event-specific rules.
- **Sled push, sled pull:** Both athletes push/pull together for the full distance.
- **Burpee broad jump, sandbag lunge:** Athletes can split the distance (50 m each on the lunge) or one does the full distance while the other rests.
- **Farmer's carry:** Same.
- **Wall ball:** Split into 50/50 reps.

The doubles strategy guide deserves its own article (see [Hyrox doubles handoff strategy](/blog/hyrox-doubles-handoff-strategy)). The short version: pair athletes of similar fitness and agree the split per station before the race.

## Masters categories

Masters at Hyrox uses five-year age bands and scales the weights at the heavier end. The schedule varies year to year and by category. As a rough guide:

| Age band | Open men's farmer carry | Open men's sandbag |
|---|---|---|
| 35-39 | 24 kg | 20 kg |
| 40-44 | 24 kg | 20 kg |
| 45-49 | 24 kg | 20 kg |
| 50-54 | 24 kg | 15 kg |
| 55-59 | 20 kg | 15 kg |
| 60-64 | 20 kg | 10 kg |
| 65+ | 16 kg | 10 kg |

The sled weights also scale down in the 55+ categories. Check the official Hyrox rulebook for the precise schedule at your event.

## Burpee broad jump rules

The minimum jump distance is 1 metre. Athletes regularly disqualify themselves by jumping shorter and not realising it. The official judges measure each jump informally; if you're consistently under 1 m, you'll be called.

The 80 m distance equals 80 successful 1 m jumps. Tall athletes who can broad-jump 1.5 m can finish in 53 jumps. Most open athletes do 70-80.

## Wall ball depth + height

The wall ball requires:
- **Squat to parallel.** Hip crease below the knee at the bottom of the squat.
- **Ball touches the target wall.** The ball must clearly hit at or above the target line.

Failed reps are called by the judge. Most wall-ball penalties come from athletes who get tired and start half-squatting in the last 30 reps.

## The honest summary

The Hyrox weights are fixed. Train against the exact race-day weight for your category from week 6 onward. Don't train sled push at 100 kg if you'll race 152 kg. The body adapts to what you load it with; load it with the truth.

The sled weights are the most-feared in training but the most-trainable. The wall ball is the most-deciding station. Train the wall ball at race weight, race-target height, in race-tired states.

The standards exist to make your time honest. Train against them.
