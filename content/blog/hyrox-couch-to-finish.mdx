The "12-week Hyrox build" assumes you can already run 5 km and squat your bodyweight. If you can't, that plan isn't for you yet.

This is the version for someone genuinely starting from nothing. You haven't run since school. You haven't lifted weights ever. You signed up for a Hyrox 4 months from now because a friend dared you, and now you're wondering if you should pull out.

Don't pull out. You can do this. Here's the honest 16-week version.

## The realistic prerequisites

You need three things to start this build:

1. **You can walk briskly for 30 minutes** without joint pain or chest tightness.
2. **You've been cleared by a GP** for moderate-intensity exercise (no untreated heart condition, no acute injury, no pregnancy complications).
3. **You can commit 4 hours per week** for 16 weeks, three short sessions (30-45 min) plus one longer session (60-75 min) on the weekend.

If those three are true, the rest is doable.

## The 16-week structure

| Weeks | Block | Focus |
|---|---|---|
| 1-4 | Pre-base | Walk-run intervals, bodyweight strength, movement learning |
| 5-7 | Base | Continuous easy running, intro to barbell + kettlebell |
| 8-10 | Build | Hyrox-pattern introduction, race-pace work |
| 11-13 | Specific | Full Hyrox-pattern sessions, station volume |
| 14-15 | Peak | Race-pace, race-simulation |
| 16 | Taper | Cut volume, maintain intensity |

The first 4 weeks are the difference between this and a standard 12-week build. They exist because asking a beginner to run continuously in week 1 is unrealistic. Walk-run progression gets the cardiovascular system online without breaking the legs.

## Week-by-week: the first 4 weeks (pre-base)

### Week 1

**Goal:** establish the habit of training 4 times this week. Quality is secondary.

- **Monday:** 30-minute walk, brisk pace.
- **Wednesday:** 5 min walk + 6 rounds of (1 min jog + 2 min walk) + 5 min walk. ~25 min total.
- **Friday:** 15 minutes of bodyweight: 10 squats, 10 incline push-ups, 30-second plank, 10 lunges. Repeat 3 times.
- **Saturday:** 40-minute walk, brisk pace.

**What you should feel:** mildly out of breath in places. Not destroyed. Sore the next day is fine; injured is not.

### Week 2

**Goal:** repeat with slightly more running.

- **Monday:** 35-minute walk + 4 × 1-min jog intervals interspersed.
- **Wednesday:** 5 min walk + 6 rounds of (2 min jog + 1 min walk) + 5 min walk. ~30 min.
- **Friday:** Bodyweight + introduce a kettlebell or a heavy water bottle. 15 squats + 12 incline push-ups + 30-second plank + 10 walking lunges. 3 rounds.
- **Saturday:** 45-minute walk + 5 × 1-min jogs.

### Week 3

- **Monday:** 30-min easy jog with 2 walk breaks if needed.
- **Wednesday:** 4 rounds of (4 min jog + 1 min walk).
- **Friday:** Bodyweight + introduce moderate kettlebell: 12 KB swings + 12 squats + 10 push-ups + 30-sec plank. 3 rounds.
- **Saturday:** 50-minute Zone 2 walk-jog mix.

### Week 4

- **Monday:** 30-min continuous easy jog. If you can't yet, 25 min with one 2-min walk break.
- **Wednesday:** Hyrox-pattern intro: 5 min jog + 30 sec KB swings + 5 min jog + 30 sec push-ups + 5 min jog + 30 sec plank. ~22 min.
- **Friday:** Strength: 3 sets of (8 goblet squats + 8 KB deadlifts + 8 push-ups + 8 ring rows or band rows).
- **Saturday:** 60-min easy Zone 2 walk-jog. By the end of this week you should be able to jog 5 km, even if slow.

By week 4 you've built the foundation. The next 12 weeks are the standard Hyrox build.

## Weeks 5-16: the standard build (overview)

You now follow the same arc as any other Hyrox first-timer:

- **Weeks 5-7 (Base):** continuous Zone 2 runs build to 60 minutes. Strength sessions move to barbell or heavier kettlebell. One Hyrox-pattern session per week.
- **Weeks 8-10 (Build):** race-pace intervals introduced. Hyrox-pattern sessions add station weight.
- **Weeks 11-13 (Specific):** weekly Hyrox-pattern at race weight. Long aerobic still in place.
- **Weeks 14-15 (Peak):** race-simulation sessions. Two-thirds-distance race rehearsals.
- **Week 16 (Taper):** cut volume by 40-50%, keep intensity. Sleep. Carb-load on Thursday-Friday. Race weekend.

## What to expect at the finish line

A first-timer finishing on a 16-week beginner build:

- **Time:** 110-125 minutes for most. Sub-110 if you had a fitness base. 130+ if you struggled with the build but made it through.
- **Feel:** broken but standing. Most first-timers describe feeling proud, slightly tearful, surprised they did it.
- **Recovery:** sore for 3-5 days. Walking only for 2 days post-race. Easy training back by day 7.

It's not glamorous. It's not Instagrammable. It is genuinely transformative. About 70% of first-time Hyrox finishers sign up for a second within 6 months.

## The 5 mistakes that wreck beginner builds

**1. Skipping the pre-base block.** "I'm fit enough to start the 12-week plan." Maybe, but the cost of injury in week 6 sets you back 4-8 weeks. The pre-base is cheap insurance.

**2. Trying to add weight before the form is solid.** Heavy goblet squats with bad form leads to back pain. The first 4 weeks of strength work is movement quality, not load.

**3. Treating recovery as optional.** You're a beginner; your body adapts slower than an experienced lifter. Sleep matters more for you, not less.

**4. Going to a Hyrox class instead of following the plan.** Hyrox-themed classes are race-pace from day 1. Beginners need to build up. Take one Hyrox class per month max in the first 8 weeks; the rest of the week is your plan.

**5. Comparison.** Don't compare yourself to the people in the gym who've been at this for years. Compare yourself to week-1 you. That's the only relevant baseline.

## What you'll need

The honest list:

- One pair of hybrid trainers (£80-130). Most important purchase.
- A water bottle.
- Comfortable training kit you've worn before.
- A timer or watch.
- Access to a gym 1-2 days/week (commercial gym is fine. PureGym, Nuffield, JD Gyms).
