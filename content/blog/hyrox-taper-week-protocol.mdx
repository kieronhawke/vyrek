The taper is the most counterintuitive week of the entire Hyrox build. You've spent 11 weeks training hard. The instinct is to push harder in week 12, one more long run, one more PR sled session, one final wall ball test.

That instinct is wrong. The taper week's job is to deliver intact what you've built. Not to add anything. Not to test anything.

This is the protocol we use for every athlete on every Vyrek programme. It's also what most plateau athletes get wrong in their second or third race.

## The principle: cut volume, keep intensity

A taper isn't a rest. A rest is what week 8 is for. A taper is a *controlled reduction* with two specific rules:

1. **Volume drops by 40-50%.** If your usual week is 4-5 hours of training, taper week is 2-2.5 hours.
2. **Intensity stays high in two short sessions.** Two race-pace or above-race-pace efforts of 20-30 minutes each.

Why this works: aerobic capacity holds for 7-10 days after the last hard session. Strength holds for 10-14 days. What clears in 7 days is fatigue. The taper week removes the fatigue without removing the fitness.

## The 7-day plan

Below is a sample taper week for an athlete training 5 days per week during the build. Scale proportionally if you train 4 or 6 days normally.

### Monday, easy aerobic

- **Workout:** 30-minute Zone 2 run.
- **Why:** keeps the legs moving without taxing glycogen.
- **Quality cue:** if you could carry a conversation, you're going the right speed.

### Tuesday, short race-pace intervals

- **Workout:** 4 × 800 m at goal Hyrox pace, 90 seconds rest between.
- **Why:** primes the neuromuscular system. Reminds your body what race pace feels like.
- **Quality cue:** the splits should feel "smooth" not "redline". If you're gasping by rep 3, you went too hard, slow down.

### Wednesday. REST

- No training.
- Light walk if your nerves are climbing.
- Foam roll for 10 minutes if you want.

### Thursday. Hyrox-pattern micro-session

- **Workout:** 20-minute Hyrox-pattern at moderate intensity.
 - 800 m run + 30-second sled push
 - 800 m run + 30-second wall balls
 - 800 m run + 30-second burpee broad jumps
- **Why:** rehearses the rhythm of the race without taxing capacity.
- **Quality cue:** moderate intensity, not race-pace. Heart rate 70-80% of max, not 90%.

### Friday, mobility + carb-load starts

- **Workout:** 20-minute mobility + breathing.
- **Nutrition:** carb intake bumps to 8-10 g per kg of bodyweight. Familiar foods. No alcohol.

### Saturday. REST + travel (if needed)

- No training.
- If you're travelling to the race venue, do it today not Sunday morning.
- Walk the venue from the outside if it's open. Find the entrance, the bag drop, the parking. Build familiarity.
- Bed by 9 PM.

### Sunday. RACE DAY

Wake up. [Run the 90-minute warm-up protocol](/blog/hyrox-race-day-warm-up). Execute the plan. Don't try anything new.

## What NOT to do during taper week

These are the most common taper-week mistakes:

**1. The "test" session.** "Just one more sled push session to check my time", no. You won't gain fitness in week 12 and you might wreck your legs.

**2. The long run.** "I'll just do a 90-minute Zone 2 to keep the engine going", no. Long aerobic work in the final week burns glycogen you need for race day.

**3. New shoes.** Don't break in new shoes during taper. Race in shoes you've trained in for at least three sessions.

**4. New supplements.** Don't try a new pre-workout, gel, or electrolyte product the week of the race. Race-week stomachs are sensitive.

**5. New nutrition pattern.** Don't suddenly switch to keto or carnivore the week before. Eat what you've trained on.

**6. The "I haven't done enough" panic session.** This is the most insidious. Around day 3-4 of taper you'll feel flat, heavy, and convinced you've lost fitness. **You haven't.** That feeling is the taper working, adaptation is kicking in. Trust it.

## The taper-week feel

A normal taper week feels like this:

- **Day 1-2:** Slightly antsy, legs feel okay, mood fine.
- **Day 3-4:** Flat, sluggish, weirdly heavy legs. Convinced you've lost fitness. **This is good, it's the taper working.**
- **Day 5-6:** Springy. Sleep improves. Heart rate at rest drops. Mood lifts.
- **Day 7:** Sharp. Alert. Slightly fizzy. This is peak.

If you arrive at day 7 feeling sluggish, you didn't taper enough. If you arrive at day 7 feeling exhausted, you over-trained in weeks 10-11. If you arrive at day 7 feeling sharp, you executed the taper correctly.

## What to do the night before

- Pack tonight. Race kit, bib, timing chip, fuel, spare socks.
- Charge your watch.
- Set the alarm for 90 minutes before your wave + travel buffer.
- Phone do-not-disturb on.
- Eat dinner 3-4 hours before bed: familiar carb-heavy meal, no alcohol.
- Bed by 9 PM. If you can't sleep, lie still in the dark, rest is almost as good as sleep.

## After the race

The post-race recovery deserves its own framework, but the short version:

- Days 1-2: complete rest. Walk only.
- Days 3-4: light mobility. Foam roll. 20-minute swim or bike if you can.
- Days 5-7: easy Zone 2 runs back in. No intervals. No stations.
- Week 2 post-race: back to a programme. If you're targeting another race, [your next 12-week build starts on the next Tuesday](/quiz).

## The takeaway

Taper week is the simplest week of the build. Cut volume, keep intensity, sleep more, eat carbs, trust the work that's done.

Most plateau athletes wreck their race in the final week by trying to add fitness that can't be added. Don't be that athlete. The race is already there in your legs. Your only job is to deliver it.
