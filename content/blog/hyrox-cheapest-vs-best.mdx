The Hyrox training market in the UK looks like this:

- **Free**, downloadable PDFs from PureGym, Gymshark, various gym blogs
- **£8.99/mo**. Vyrek (us, software-first with weekly recalibration)
- **£30/mo**. Marchon (Hyrox-specific, more programmes, in-app community)
- **£45/mo**. Hybrid Athlete Club (premium, athlete-led community)
- **£150-400/mo**. 1:1 coaching with named human coaches

Each tier sells you something genuinely different. Here's the honest comparison so you can pick the right one, including the cases where the cheaper option is actually better.

## What each tier actually delivers

### Free PDF

**What you get:** A 12-week plan as a static PDF. Daily sessions written out: run distance, station work, rest. Typically generic, same plan for every reader.

**What you don't get:** Personalisation, recalibration, adjustment for missed sessions, audio-prompted workouts, equipment-aware substitutions.

**Best for:** Your first Hyrox if you're disciplined and have a strong fitness base. The plan structure is fine; you just have to do the work without any feedback loop.

**Watch for:** Generic plans don't know your race date, equipment, injuries, or body weight. You'll have to interpret the plan against your reality.

### Vyrek (£8.99/mo)

**What you get:** Personalised 12-week programme from a 3-minute quiz. All 4 programmes (First Race, Sub-90, Doubles, Pro) included. Sunday recalibration based on what you logged. Equipment-aware substitutions. Calibrated loads for your sex + body weight. First-week-free trial.

**What you don't get:** Human coach messaging you. Video form check on demand. Custom block written for your name.

**Best for:** First-race finishers, second-race PR-chasers, plateau-break athletes, doubles teams. Anyone for whom £30+/mo isn't worth the marginal programming improvement.

**Honest framing:** Software-first programming, not 1:1 coaching. We say this explicitly. The programming logic is identical to what a £150/mo coach would write, applied to your inputs, updated weekly. The difference is human accountability, which costs more elsewhere.

### Marchon (~£30/mo Core, £40/mo Pro)

**What you get:** Hyrox-specific app with multiple programmes, in-app community, structured tracking, polished UX. 7-day trial with 3-month minimum after.

**What you don't get:** Personalisation as deep as Vyrek's (Marchon picks from preset blocks). Single race-date calibration depth.

**Best for:** Athletes who want a more app-native experience, community feeds, leaderboards, and don't mind the 3-month commitment.

**Watch for:** The 3-month minimum is buried in the fine print. Cancellation is harder than the marketing suggests.

### Hybrid Athlete Club (£45/mo)

**What you get:** Premium Hyrox-focused programmes from named elite athletes, in-app community, race calculator tools, more elite-track athlete vibe.

**What you don't get:** As mass-market as Marchon. As cheap as Vyrek.

**Best for:** Athletes with Pro-division ambition who want to train alongside a community at that level.

**Watch for:** Higher price reflects positioning as much as substance. Programming quality is excellent; whether you need the price point depends on your trajectory.

### 1:1 coaching (£150-400/mo)

**What you get:** A named human coach who writes blocks specifically for you, messages you weekly, watches your video uploads, adjusts on demand. Real accountability.

**What you don't get:** Software automation. Plans regenerate when you change inputs. You'll wait for the coach.

**Best for:** Elite-track athletes targeting Worlds qualification. Athletes with complex injury histories. Athletes for whom human accountability is the conversion mechanism, not the programming.

**Watch for:** Quality varies enormously. A bad £200/mo coach is worse than a good £5/mo app.

## The honest decision tree

**You've never raced Hyrox.** Vyrek or a free PDF. The 12-week structure matters more than the platform, both will get you to a finish line. Vyrek's edge is real-week-day calibration; the free PDF's edge is no monthly cost. If you have £20 of total budget for race-prep, spend it on Vyrek; if you have zero, the PDF works.

**You've raced once and want to break 100 minutes.** Vyrek. The 12-week recalibration is exactly what closes a 5-10 minute gap on second-race time. Free PDFs don't adapt.

**You've raced multiple times and are stuck at 90-95 minutes.** Vyrek Sub-90 programme or a £30+/mo competitor. Both will work. The question is whether you need the community / app polish that the higher tier brings, for many athletes, the answer is no.

**You're chasing sub-80 or Pro qualification.** Vyrek Pro + 1:1 coaching consult. The £8.99/mo programme handles the structure; a £100-200 monthly 1:1 with a Pro-level coach handles your specific limiters and the qualifying tactics.

**You're racing doubles.** Vyrek Doubles programme. There's almost no doubles-specific programming on the market, most apps treat doubles as "two athletes doing the solo plan together," which is wrong. Vyrek's Doubles programme is built around handoff strategy and split-pacing decisions.

## What price doesn't buy

A few honest notes about what more money doesn't get you:

- **Better aerobic base.** Aerobic capacity comes from consistent volume. No app delivers more of that per pound spent than another.
- **Race-day pacing discipline.** Your watch and your willpower do this. No coach can race for you.
- **Sleep, nutrition, recovery.** £8.99/mo or £400/mo, the same 8 hours of sleep matters either way.

What price *does* buy:

- **Programming density.** Higher-tier apps have more workout templates, more variation.
- **Human accountability.** Real coaches text you. Apps notify you.
- **Community.** Some athletes train better surrounded by other people on the same journey.
- **Edge-case handling.** Complex injuries, masters with multiple conditions, returning athletes after long breaks, human coaches earn their fee here.

Because the programming can be delivered by software at scale, and we'd rather have 10,000 paying members at £5 than 1,000 at £45. The honesty point ([Why £8.99?](/pricing)) is that we're a software platform, not 1:1 coaching. Most members never need 1:1.

## The cohort that gets it wrong

The most expensive mistake we see: athletes paying £45/mo for a premium app, then not doing the prescribed sessions. The £45/mo app doesn't make you run on Tuesday. Discipline does that. Without it, the programming tier is irrelevant.

Cheapest setup that works: a £8.99/mo plan + a watch + a wall to push a sled into + the willingness to show up. That beats any £400/mo coaching package whose sessions you skip.
