How you split your training week matters more than how many hours you put in. Five hard sessions stacked back-to-back will break you. Four sessions spread with proper recovery will compound for 12 weeks.

## The four session types

Every Hyrox training week is built from some combination of:

1. **Aerobic base.** Easy continuous running, cycling, swimming, rowing. 40-90 minutes at conversational pace. Builds the engine.
2. **Strength.** Compound lifts (deadlift, squat, press) plus accessories. 50-60 minutes. Builds the chassis.
3. **Station-specific work.** Sled push, wall ball EMOMs, sandbag lunges. 40-55 minutes. Builds the specific muscular endurance.
4. **Race simulation.** A condensed version of race day. 45-70 minutes. Tests the integration.

A good Hyrox week includes all four. A great Hyrox week orders them so each session sets up the next.

## The 3-day week

For athletes with limited time. Race-finishable, not race-fast.

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Tue | Strength + 30 min easy run |
| Thu | Station-specific (sled + wall ball EMOM) |
| Sat | Race simulation OR long aerobic |

Rest days are full rest, not active recovery. Maximises the 3 hard sessions you do have.

## The 4-day week (most athletes)

The sweet spot for finishers targeting a strong first race or sub-90.

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Mon | Aerobic intervals (4 × 1 km at race pace) |
| Tue | Strength (compound + accessory) |
| Thu | Station-specific work |
| Sat | Race simulation OR long aerobic (alternate weekly) |

Wednesday is active recovery (mobility, easy walk). Friday is full rest. Sunday is full rest.

## The 5-day week

For athletes with a strong endurance base targeting sub-80 or competitive masters categories.

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Mon | Aerobic intervals |
| Tue | Strength |
| Wed | Easy aerobic 45-60 min |
| Thu | Station-specific |
| Sat | Race simulation |

Friday active recovery. Sunday off or easy walk.

## The 6-day week (advanced)

Only sustainable for athletes who:
- Sleep 8+ hours every night
- Have a stable life (no shift work, no young kids, etc.)
- Are in week 6+ of a planned build

| Day | Session |
|---|---|
| Mon | Aerobic intervals |
| Tue | Strength |
| Wed | Easy aerobic |
| Thu | Station-specific |
| Fri | Skill / technique (sled push pyramid, ski erg pace work) |
| Sat | Race simulation |

Sunday off. Mandatory.

## The ordering principle

The hardest session should be the most recovered session. That usually means:

- **Saturday = race simulation** (recovered from Friday off)
- **Tuesday = strength** (recovered from Monday's quality, or from Sunday off)
- **Thursday = station-specific** (recovered from Wednesday's easier day)

Avoid putting two demanding sessions back-to-back. Avoid putting race simulation the day before strength. Avoid Friday + Saturday hard combos.

## What goes wrong

Three common training-week mistakes:

**1. Too many race simulations.** One per week. A race simulation is 70-90 minutes of near-race intensity. Two per week and you're under-recovering.

**2. Skipping the easy aerobic.** Easy aerobic feels too easy to "matter". It's the day that builds the engine. Cut it and your race-pace work gets harder, not easier.

**3. Adding extra runs.** "I'll just run 5 km on Wednesday for fun." That 5 km is now Wednesday's training session and you under-recover for Thursday. Hyrox builds use 3-4 runs per week, no more.

## Mixing it with life

Most athletes can't train at exactly the same time every day. Two principles to make it work:

- **Protect the hardest sessions.** The race simulation and the strength session get priority on the calendar.
- **Move sessions, don't skip them.** A Tuesday session moved to Wednesday is fine. A skipped Tuesday is a lost adaptation.

If you have a busy week and have to drop a session, drop one of the easy ones (Wednesday aerobic, Friday skill). Keep the hard ones.

## Weekly load distribution

Across the week, the cumulative training load should look something like:

| Day | Load |
|---|---|
| Mon | 70% |
| Tue | 80% |
| Wed | 30% |
| Thu | 70% |
| Fri | 20% |
| Sat | 90% |
| Sun | 0% |

That distribution gives the body a chance to absorb hard sessions before the next one. Stacking 90% days back-to-back is how injuries happen.

## The build phases

A 12-week Hyrox build typically structures the weeks differently:

- **Weeks 1-4 (base):** 3-4 day weeks. Heavy on aerobic base. Light on intensity.
- **Weeks 5-8 (build):** 4-5 day weeks. Add intensity. Add race-specific.
- **Weeks 9-11 (peak):** 5-6 day weeks. Highest intensity. Race simulations weekly.
- **Week 12 (taper):** 3-4 day week. Cut volume 40-60%. Keep intensity sharp.

The week structure changes as the build progresses. Don't try to start at 6 days; build into it.

## The honest summary

The right training week depends on your goal, your recovery capacity, and your life. Most athletes thrive on a 4-day week with proper rest days. Trying to train 6 days when your sleep won't support it is the fastest way to under-recover.

Pick the week that you can sustain for 12 weeks. Adjust based on how you feel in week 4. The athletes who race well are the ones whose training week is sustainable, not heroic.
