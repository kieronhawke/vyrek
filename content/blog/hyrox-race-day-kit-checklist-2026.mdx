A clear race-day kit list saves stress and time. This is what experienced Hyrox athletes pack, what's worth investing in, and what the marketing departments would like you to buy but won't help your race.

## The essential checklist

What you cannot race without:

- **Hyrox-compatible shoes.** Cross-trainers, not running shoes.
- **Shorts or leggings.** Whatever you train in.
- **Top.** T-shirt or vest.
- **Sports bra (for women).** High-support; you'll be jumping and lifting.
- **Race bib + chip.** Issued at registration.
- **ID + entry confirmation.** Have both physical and phone copies.
- **Water bottle.** Most events have water stations but a 500 ml bottle for the warm-up.

That's the floor. You can race Hyrox with just this.

## The shoes question

Shoes matter most. Three categories, in order of suitability:

**1. Cross-trainers (best).** Built for mixed activities. Moderate cushion, lateral stability, flat or low heel-toe drop.

Recommended: Reebok Nano X4, Nike Metcon 9, Inov-8 F-Lite G 300, NoBull Trainer, Born Primitive Savage 1.

**2. Trail / hybrid runners (acceptable).** More cushion than cross-trainers, slightly less lateral support. Work fine for the running but feel less stable on the sled.

Recommended: Nike Wildhorse, Inov-8 TrailFly, Salomon Speedcross (only on dry surfaces).

**3. Pure running shoes (avoid).** Maximum cushion, narrow lateral base. Great for marathons. Bad for sled pushes.

What to avoid:
- Weightlifting shoes (elevated heel kills the running)
- Minimalist barefoot shoes (no cushion for 8 km)
- Brand new shoes you haven't trained in (blisters guaranteed)

## What to wear

The weather inside a Hyrox arena is warmer than outside. Even in winter, the arena temperature climbs into the low 20s C with thousands of athletes producing heat. Dress for warmer than the outdoor temperature.

**Bottoms:**
- Shorts (preferred): light fabric, mid-length, no zip pockets.
- Leggings (acceptable): if you train in them. Tight-fit so the bag and sandbag don't catch.
- Avoid loose tracksuit bottoms; they catch on equipment.

**Tops:**
- T-shirt or vest, breathable fabric.
- Sports bra (women): high-support, ideally racerback. The wall ball + run combo punishes low-support bras.
- Avoid cotton; gets heavy with sweat.

**Accessories:**
- Headband or hair tie. Hair in eyes during wall ball is a known issue.
- Wristbands (optional). Some athletes like to wipe sweat between stations.

## What to bring in your bag

For the race itself + after:

- **Race kit (above)** packed the night before.
- **Recovery kit:** dry clothes (full change), towel, deodorant.
- **Food:** pre-race meal eaten before arrival, plus a recovery shake or banana for immediately post-race.
- **Hydration:** 500-750 ml bottle pre-race, refill stations available.
- **Phone + charger.** For race photos and post-race travel.
- **Cash + card.** For event merchandise or transport.
- **Hand sanitizer + tissues.** Self-explanatory.

## Optional kit (the "if it helps" list)

These are nice-to-have but won't change your race:

- **Foam roller or massage ball.** For the post-race car or train.
- **Compression socks / sleeves.** Marginal performance benefit, decent recovery aid.
- **Caffeine tablets or pre-workout drink.** Tested in training.
- **Energy gel (single).** For mid-race if you're worried about fuelling.
- **Light gloves.** For the sled pull rope grip.

## What NOT to bring

Save your stress and your weight:

- **Heart rate monitor / watch.** You won't have time to check it during stations.
- **Lifting belt.** None of the Hyrox loads need one. It restricts breathing.
- **Lifting straps.** Most events ban them on the farmer's carry.
- **Headphones.** Banned during the race.
- **Bulky jacket / hoodie.** Wear something easy to drop at the bag-check.

## Pre-race day prep

The night before:

1. **Lay out the kit.** Everything you'll wear, in the order you'll put it on.
2. **Pack the bag.** Recovery kit, food, water, valuables.
3. **Pin the race bib to your top.** Now, not in the morning when you're stressed.
4. **Charge the phone.**
5. **Set 2 alarms.**

The morning:

1. **Eat 2-3 hours before race start.** Real meal, low fibre.
2. **Arrive 60-75 minutes before race start.** Time for warm-up + bag-check + bathroom.
3. **Bathroom one final time 15 minutes before lining up.**

## Warm-up kit

A separate warm-up outfit isn't necessary but useful in cold months:

- **Light jacket** to wear during pre-race warm-up. Removed before the start.
- **Beanie/buff** if outdoors briefly.

You can put these in the bag-check area before the race starts.

## What happens at registration

Most events:
1. Show ID + entry email.
2. Receive bib + chip (one or both).
3. Receive bag-check bracelet.
4. Bag-check your stuff before the race.

Allow 20-30 minutes for registration. Arrive 90 minutes early if it's your first time so you're not stressed.

## Race-day common mistakes

- **Wearing new shoes.** Blister city. Race in shoes you've trained in for at least 4 weeks.
- **Eating something new for breakfast.** GI upset 30 minutes in. Stick to what you've tested.
- **Underdressing because it's cold outside.** Arena gets hot. You'll overheat.
- **Forgetting the bib pinning.** They check at the start line.
- **Not refilling the water bottle.** Aid stations are crowded; carry your own.

## Post-race

Within 60 minutes:
- **Carbohydrate + protein.** Recovery shake, sandwich, banana.
- **Water.** 500-1000 ml with electrolytes.
- **Dry change of clothes.** The post-race chill is real.

Within 6 hours:
- Real meal. Steak, rice, vegetables.
- 30 minutes easy walking. Helps drain lactate.

Within 24 hours:
- Full rest day.
- 9+ hours sleep.

## The honest summary

Hyrox doesn't require a £500 kit haul. Decent cross-trainers (£100-150), shorts and a top you already own, a high-support sports bra (£40-60), and you're set.

Test everything in training. Don't experiment on race day. The kit you pack the night before is the kit that gives you a stress-free race morning.

The race is decided by your training. The kit just keeps you comfortable enough to use it.
