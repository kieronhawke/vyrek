A specific warm-up protocol for Hyrox raises your race-day performance by 3-5%. That's 2-5 minutes off your finish time at most paces. A generic "I'll just jog around" doesn't.

This is the 30-minute protocol that primes the body, the nervous system, and the mind for race start.

## The minute-by-minute protocol

| Time before race | Activity |
|---|---|
| -45 min | Final small snack (gel or banana) |
| -35 min | General warm-up: light jog 5 min |
| -28 min | Dynamic mobility: 5 min |
| -22 min | Activation: 3-4 movements, 2 sets each |
| -15 min | Station rehearsals: 2 movements, light |
| -10 min | Opener: 2 short intervals at race pace |
| -6 min | Easy walk + breathing + mental cues |
| -2 min | Line up. Race-start posture. |

Total time: ~33 minutes. Adjust if your event has different staging.

## -35 to -30 min: light jog

5 minutes of easy jogging. Heart rate climbs to 100-120 bpm. Body warms up. Mental switch flips from "preparing" to "active".

Use the venue's warm-up space if there is one. If not, the carpark or any flat open space works.

## -28 to -23 min: dynamic mobility

A standard sequence:

- **Walking lunges with twist** × 10 each leg
- **High knees** × 30 seconds
- **Butt kicks** × 30 seconds
- **Leg swings (front, side, cross)** × 10 each direction, each leg
- **Arm circles + ski erg motion practice** × 1 min
- **Hip circles** × 5 each direction

Total: 5 minutes. Targets: hips, hamstrings, shoulders, ankles. Skip stretching anything (static stretching cold-stiffens muscles for explosive work).

## -22 to -16 min: activation

Specific muscle activation for the patterns the race demands:

- **Glute bridge** × 15 reps
- **Hip thrust (bodyweight or single-leg)** × 12 reps
- **Plank** × 30 seconds
- **Push-up to T-rotation** × 8 reps (4 each side)
- **Air squat** × 15 reps

Two rounds. 6 minutes total. These prime the posterior chain and core for sled work, lunges, and wall ball.

## -15 to -11 min: station rehearsals

If equipment is available, do light rehearsals of the early stations:

- **Ski erg:** 30 seconds at moderate intensity. Just to feel the motion.
- **Light sled push** (50% race weight, 10 m): if available, one short push.

If equipment isn't available (often the case): pantomime the motion. Standing ski erg pull with hands miming. Light bodyweight squats. The neuromuscular pattern still primes.

## -10 to -6 min: opener intervals

The most important part of the warm-up. Two short race-pace efforts to wake up the cardiovascular system.

- **30 seconds at race pace running.** (Could be on the spot if no space.)
- **90 seconds easy walk.**
- **20 seconds slightly faster than race pace.**
- **2 minutes easy walk.**

These openers raise blood lactate to about 4 mmol/L (the threshold zone), which then clears before race start. The first 1 km of the race feels easier because the system has been pre-shocked.

## -6 to -2 min: breathing + mental cues

Settle. Breathing slow and deep. Mental script:

- "I've trained for this."
- "First 1 km is conservative. Find rhythm."
- "Stations 1-3 at 75-80%. Save the back half."
- "Wall ball is decided by how I pace the first half."

Repeat your race plan to yourself. Visualise the first three stations.

Sip 100-150 ml of water. Not more. Last bathroom should be done by now.

## -2 to 0 min: line up

Position yourself near the front of your wave. Don't be at the back; you'll waste energy weaving around slower athletes in the first 50 m.

Posture: tall, light on the feet, slow deep breaths. Don't bounce around expending energy.

## What to skip

Common warm-up mistakes:

**1. Long static stretching.** Cold muscles don't benefit. Stiffens explosive muscle.

**2. Heavy warm-up sets at the sled.** You're racing in 15 minutes. Save the heavy work.

**3. Full station rehearsals at race weight.** Tempting because the kit is there. Wastes glycogen.

**4. Stretching the same muscle for 60 seconds.** Reduces power output for 30+ minutes.

**5. Standing around for 20 minutes after warming up.** The body cools down. Re-do a brief activation if you've been waiting too long.

## If your wave is delayed

Sometimes events run late. If your wave is pushed back 30+ minutes:

- **Don't re-do the full warm-up.** You'll burn out.
- **Move every 10 minutes.** Easy jog 60-90 seconds, light squats, arm circles.
- **Re-do the openers (30s + 20s race-pace efforts) in the 5 minutes before your actual start.**

If the delay is shorter (10-15 min), keep moving and re-do one opener.

## Caffeine timing

For athletes who use caffeine:
- **Dose:** 3-6 mg per kg body weight.
- **Timing:** 30-60 minutes before race start.
- **Form:** Coffee, espresso, caffeine tablets, or pre-workout drink. Test in training first.
- **Don't experiment on race day.**

Caffeine peaks 30-45 minutes after ingestion. If you ingest at -60 minutes, you'll be at peak effect for the first 30-45 minutes of the race. Plan accordingly.

## Race-morning fuelling

Reminder of the timing (covered fully in the [nutrition timeline article](/blog/hyrox-12-week-nutrition-timeline)):

- **3 hours before:** Real meal, 80-120 g carbs.
- **45 minutes before:** 30-50 g fast carbs (gel or banana).
- **15 minutes before:** Final sip of water.

If you skip the final small snack, the first 1 km feels harder.

## The mental warm-up

The 30 minutes before race start are also a mental rehearsal. While you do the physical protocol:

- Visualise the start line.
- Picture the ski erg pace you'll hold.
- See yourself executing the sled push posture.
- Imagine the wall ball station and the rhythm you'll keep.

Athletes who rehearse the race mentally perform 3-5% better on race day. The brain treats vivid visualisation as semi-real practice.

## The honest summary

A specific warm-up beats a generic one by 2-5 minutes of finish time. The protocol: light jog, dynamic mobility, activation, station rehearsal, opener intervals, breathing.

Total time: 30-35 minutes. Skip the static stretching, the heavy lifts, the over-rehearsed station work. The warm-up exists to prime, not to test.

Race-day performance is 90% training and 10% the day-of decisions. The warm-up is a big chunk of the 10%.
