You've signed up for your first Hyrox. You've read the Reddit threads. The recommendations include knee sleeves (£40), a lifting belt (£60), grip pads (£25), compression socks (£35), a £150 hybrid trainer, a £80 race kit, and a pre-workout supplement stack (£90).

You don't need any of that. Here's what a first-timer actually needs, why, and what to skip.

## The honest first-timer's kit list

**Total budget: £140 if you don't already own decent trainers. £30 if you do.**

### Shoes (£80-130)

The one piece of kit that genuinely matters. You need a hybrid trainer, low drop (4-8 mm), firm midsole, lateral stability, grippy non-marking outsole. Popular categories:

- **Nike Metcon 9**, £130. Stiff, reliable, durable. The default for many racers.
- **Reebok Nano X4**, £110. Slightly more cushioned, good for runners crossing over.
- **NoBull Trainer**, £130. Sleek, popular in the UK Hyrox community.
- **Tyr CXT-2**, £100. Newer brand, designed specifically for hybrid sport.

Avoid: maximalist running shoes (Vaporfly, Hoka Bondi), weightlifting shoes, minimalist barefoot shoes. Full breakdown in our [Hyrox shoe guide](/hyrox/gear/best-hyrox-shoes).

**If you already own a hybrid trainer:** race in it. New shoes on race day is a mistake. Race in something you've trained at least 3 sessions in.

### Training kit (£0-50)

You don't need a Hyrox-branded race kit. You need workout clothes you've sweated in before, that fit, and that you can run + push a sled in.

- Shorts or leggings, whatever you train in. Pockets are useful.
- Tech tee or vest, quick-dry, no cotton.
- Sports bra (where relevant), high support; you're running plus jumping.

Race in the kit you've trained in. Don't break in a new outfit on race day.

### Socks (£10-15)

Most underrated kit item. The wrong socks cause blisters by km 5.

- Thin merino blend or synthetic. Stance, Balega, Injinji, Lululemon Power Stride.
- Snug fit at the arch.
- Low to mid cuff.
- Brand-new socks bad, wear them in for 2-3 training sessions first.

Avoid pure cotton.

### Hydration + fuel (£10)

- One electrolyte tab (SaltStick, Precision Hydration, Hi5) for your warm-up bottle. £5 for a tube.
- One banana or two dates for 30 min pre-gun. £2.
- Water bottle (you probably own one). £0.
- Optional: half a gel for 15 min pre-gun if you've trained with them. £2.

That's it. No pre-workout. No nitric oxide stack. No BCAAs. The £8.99/mo Vyrek programme + a banana will beat someone in the same fitness range using a £90 supplement stack.

### Watch (optional, £0)

If you already have a Garmin / Coros / Apple Watch / Polar, wear it. It's useful for pacing the runs. If you don't, don't buy one for your first race. A wall clock at the start of each kilometre on the track is enough.

### Race-day extras (£0-15)

- Race bib + safety pins or magnetic clips (provided at check-in).
- Timing chip (provided).
- Small towel for the warm-up area (£0, you own one).
- Snack for after the race (£3).

## What to skip for your first Hyrox

These items get recommended by gear blogs but add zero value for first-timers:

**Lifting belt (£60).** Not needed for first-time race-day loads. Restricts breathing on the run. [More detail.](/hyrox/gear/best-hyrox-belt)

**Knee sleeves (£40).** Restricts running mechanics over 8 km. Only useful with a known knee issue. [More detail.](/hyrox/gear/best-hyrox-knee-sleeves)

**Gloves (£25).** Most experienced racers don't wear them. Hurt grip on kettlebells and wall balls. [More detail.](/hyrox/gear/best-hyrox-gloves)

**Compression socks (£35).** Marketing claims about blood flow are overstated. Tight calf wear can restrict over a long race.

**Wrist wraps (£20).** Hyrox movements don't require them.

**Branded race kit (£80).** A plain technical tee performs identically.

**Pre-workout supplements (£40).** Caffeine in coffee at the right dose (3-6 mg/kg, 45 min pre-race) does the same job for £2.

**Recovery boots (£300).** Lovely if you own them. Genuinely not necessary.

## Race-week purchases to make

If you're 2 weeks out and want to upgrade something, in order of impact:

1. **Better socks** if you've been racing in cotton. Single best £15 you'll spend.
2. **Replace worn-out trainers** if your current pair is 400+ km in. Pick one of the hybrid trainers above and break them in for 3 short sessions before the race.
3. **Electrolyte tabs** for the warm-up and post-race.
4. **A racing cap** if you sweat into your eyes (the wall balls + sandbag lunges drip sweat down the face).

That's the list. Total cost: under £40 if you already have shoes.

## What experienced racers actually wear

If you spectate at a UK Hyrox event, this is what you'll see on most age-group athletes:

- Hybrid trainers (any of the four mentioned)
- Plain technical shorts + a vest
- Synthetic socks
- A timing chip and a race bib
- No gloves, no belt, no sleeves
- Maybe a cap or headband
- A watch (most have one, some don't)

That's it. The gear isn't the differentiator. The training is.

## Where to go from here

You can finish your first Hyrox on £40 of kit (if you own shoes) or £140 (if you don't). The rest is marketing. Save the budget for the race entry, the travel, and possibly a hotel walking distance from the venue.
