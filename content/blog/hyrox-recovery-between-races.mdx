Racing Hyrox multiple times per year requires a recovery framework. Without it, athletes get faster for two races and slower for the third. With it, they peak twice or three times in a calendar year.

## The recovery curve

A Hyrox race (90 minutes near-max) triggers measurable physiological stress:

- **Day 1-2:** Maximum muscle soreness. Lactate clearance. Cortisol elevated.
- **Day 3-4:** Soreness decreasing. Aerobic capacity reduced 10-15%.
- **Day 5-7:** Functional recovery. Mood returning. Light training feels good.
- **Day 8-14:** Full recovery. Strength returning. Training adaptation possible again.
- **Day 14-21:** Super-compensation window. The body is over-recovered and ready to absorb a new training block.

Race again before day 14 and you're racing on incomplete recovery. Strong athletes can do it; their times slip 3-8%. Less-trained athletes get hurt.

## The week-by-week framework

After a race, the recovery week looks like:

| Day | Activity |
|---|---|
| Race | Race + 30 min walk cool-down + warm meal |
| +1 | Full rest. Easy walk. Sleep 9+ hours. |
| +2 | Full rest. Easy stretching. Walk. |
| +3 | Easy walk 45-60 min. Mobility. Light yoga. |
| +4 | Easy aerobic 30-45 min (cycle, swim, walk-jog). |
| +5 | Light strength: bodyweight or 50% normal load. |
| +6 | Easy aerobic 45-60 min. |
| +7 | First "real" session: aerobic intervals at 80% normal pace. |

By day 14, full training resumes.

## When to push the recovery

You can push the recovery shorter (10 days instead of 14) if:

- You finished the race comfortably (not all-out)
- You slept 8+ hours every night since
- You're under 35 with no injury history
- You're peaking for a target race within 4 weeks

Sample: Race Saturday, race again the following Saturday 14 days later. Possible if both efforts are paced conservatively. Not possible if both are PB attempts.

## When to back off

Extend recovery to 21 days if:

- You're a masters athlete (40+)
- You had an injury flare during or after the race
- You're sleeping less than 7 hours nightly
- You felt "off" before the race (suggesting under-recovery going in)
- You're not targeting a near-future race

A longer recovery doesn't lose fitness. It builds it; the body adapts during rest, not work.

## The 3-race year framework

For an amateur targeting 3 Hyrox per year:

| Block | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Build 1 | 12 weeks | Race 1: spring (Mar/Apr) |
| Recovery + base | 4 weeks | Active recovery + base aerobic |
| Build 2 | 8 weeks | Race 2: summer (Jun/Jul) |
| Recovery + base | 4 weeks | Active recovery + base aerobic |
| Build 3 | 10 weeks | Race 3: autumn (Sep/Oct) |
| Off-season | 8 weeks | Strength + variety (cycle, hike, lift) |

Total: ~12 months. Three race peaks. One full off-season for repair.

## The 5-race year framework

For an athlete targeting qualifier-window races:

| Block | Duration | Purpose |
|---|---|---|
| Build 1 | 10 weeks | Race 1: October |
| Recovery + maintenance | 2 weeks | Active recovery |
| Build 2 | 6 weeks | Race 2: December |
| Recovery + maintenance | 2 weeks | Active recovery |
| Build 3 | 6 weeks | Race 3: February |
| Recovery + maintenance | 2 weeks | Active recovery |
| Build 4 | 6 weeks | Race 4: April |
| Recovery + maintenance | 2 weeks | Active recovery |
| Build 5 | 6 weeks | Race 5: June (championship qualifier) |
| Off-season | 16 weeks | Full reset |

This is aggressive. Only sustainable for athletes whose life supports 50-week-per-year training.

## Signs you're under-recovered

Common markers:

- **Resting heart rate elevated 5+ bpm above baseline for 3+ days.**
- **Soreness persisting past day 4.**
- **Sleep quality dropping despite normal hours.**
- **Mood irritability.**
- **Sessions feeling harder than they should at the prescribed pace.**
- **Loss of appetite or unusual cravings.**

If three of these stack, take a full extra rest week before resuming training.

## The post-race indulgence

A common race-day question: can I drink, eat the burger, take the week off?

Yes. Within reason.

- **Race day:** Eat whatever you want. The carbohydrate refill is high-priority.
- **Race week (days 1-3):** Two drinks max. Big meals fine.
- **Race week (days 4-7):** Back to normal eating.

The athletes who derail their next training block are the ones who use the post-race recovery week as a 7-day off-the-rails period.

## The honest summary

Multi-race years are sustainable with disciplined recovery. The framework: 14 days minimum between race efforts, longer recovery for masters or under-recovered athletes, deliberate off-season at the end of the calendar.

The athletes who race well across a full year are the ones who recover deliberately. The athletes who slip into mid-season slumps are the ones who skipped the recovery and stacked training on incomplete healing.

Pick three races per year. Recover between each. The fourth race idea is usually the one that breaks you.
